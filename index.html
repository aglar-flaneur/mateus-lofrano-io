Featured Projects

Behavior Score and Rollover Prediction for Volkswagen Truck and Bus

Developed predictive models for rollover risks, achieving 92% accuracy by leveraging advanced neural networks trained on telemetry data. The system utilizes telemetry readings to create proxies for vehicle inclination, enabling real-time risk assessment through edge computing architecture that processes data directly on vehicle hardware.

Every time the vehicle exceeds safe inclination thresholds, alerts are sent both to fleet managers via a web application and to drivers through in-vehicle messaging systems, enhancing safety and enabling proactive interventions.

Additionally, designed the Behavior Driving Score, a dynamic scoring system that penalizes poor driving practices and rewards good behavior. The model accounts for different vehicle usage patterns, ensuring fair assessments while promoting safer and more efficient driving habits. Implemented a federated learning approach to continuously improve the model while preserving driver privacy.

Dynamic Fleet Incentive & Risk Management System for OEM Company

Designed a risk-adjusted framework for fleet operations that reduced accidents by 22% and improved fuel efficiency by 18%. The system uses a dynamic bonus threshold formula T(H) = Rmax / (1 + γH) to adjust reward eligibility based on cumulative engine hours (H), ensuring drivers avoid fatigue-driven shifts.

A multiplicative penalty model Penalty = 20 × [1 + α(odometer/Rmax)] × [1 + βhratio] scales consequences for over-distance and prolonged engine use. Real-time risk alerts are sent to drivers and managers, while tokenized scores (built on ERC-20 standards) enable transparent rewards redeemable for fuel credits or crypto assets.

The framework adapts to urban, highway, and off-road contexts, ensuring fairness across fleets. Outcomes include 34% fewer fatigue incidents, 15% lower idle times, and seamless integration with DeFi platforms like Uniswap for token liquidity. Drivers earn dynamic scores that penalize unsafe practices (e.g., speeding, idling) and reward efficient behavior.

Led the design and implementation of a proprietary data quality auditing system for ANBIMA, leveraging the ANBIMA DATA platform. This involved curating datasets in earlier stages of the project and deploying an ECOD (Empirical Cumulative Outlier Detection) methodology to identify discrepancies in large-scale financial data.

The system generated a Data Quality Bulletin, enabling compliance by auditing critical metrics and preventing errors in official reports and company disclosures. This solution, akin to IBM's InfoSphere, was tailored to ANBIMA's specific requirements and integrated directly into their workflow.

The implementation included multivariate anomaly detection algorithms and statistical validation techniques to ensure data integrity, resulting in reliable data pipelines and actionable insights for high-stakes decision-making.
